Making Ends Meet

People who have never been on food stamps and who don’t think they know anyone who has relied upon them think that food stamps are for people who are different from them. Food stamps are a way of coping with a challenge that almost everyone is familiar with: making ends meet. In fact, the most common dilemma GetCalFresh applicants mention in their applications is not being able to afford food after paying for rent, utilities, and gas or fare to get to work.

Cost of living

Outside observers often try to place the blame on food stamps recipients for their situation. Yet, the most common challenge that our applicants face is one that anyone living in California knows well. California’s high cost of living is a systemic problem that leaves most residents more vulnerable if they unexpectedly lose income or have an increase in expenses.

Put simply, Californians live in circumstances that make it very likely many people will need to apply for food stamps to make it through unexpected challenges. In fact, the most common sentiment expressed in our applications is the difficulty of making ends, particularly after an experiencing an adverse event. While some applicants recognize that their situation is largely a product of the high cost of living in California and bad luck, many tell us that thepoy delayed seeking food stamps because of stigma.

California has a very high cost of living that can make it difficult for families to make ends meet. Even with a full-time job, many Californians struggle to cover all their basic needs. In fact, in some California counties, the median income is lower than the cost of living (see below).
